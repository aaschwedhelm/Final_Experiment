About the City

Located amid the highest coastal mountain range in the world, Santa Marta is the third-largest city of Colombia's Caribbean coast. Due to its fast growing tourism industry, and it receiving a vast number of displaced persons, Santa Marta has experienced rapid population growth over the last three decades. This has led the city to expand quickly in the form of private middle class subdivision development and informal settlements. The City's progressive local government recently revised its landuse plan to help prevent unsustainable urban growth.
Santa Marta is the oldest European city in Colombia, which along with its touristic asstes it has been design as a touristic, cultural, and historical district.

About the Project

The urban lad cover areas of 1989, 2000, and 2013 were generated with Landsat TM 5 multispectral satellite imagery.
Urban land cover projections of 2026, 2039, and 2078 were created with LanduseSIM software with a regression model that used previous landcover growth rates, road infrastructure, and topography.
Special thanks to the LanduseSIM folks and to Geografia Urbana for providing roads and topography data
